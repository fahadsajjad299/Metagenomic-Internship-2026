# Taxonomic Biomarkers in Skin Microbiome of Atopic Dermatitis

## Study Information
- BioProject: PRJNA46333
- Study: Gene-Environment Interactions at the Skin Surface
- Samples: 50 human skin metagenome samples
- Platform: Illumina HiSeq 2000
- Location: USA
- Collection Period: June-October 2011
- KBase Narrative: MGBIO_MG_01_Fahad_Sajjad

## What is Atopic Dermatitis?
A common skin disease (Eczema) causing red, itchy, 
inflamed skin. Certain bacteria on skin trigger or 
worsen the disease.

## What are Taxonomic Biomarkers?
Bacterial signatures of a disease. If bacteria X is 
always found in sick people but not healthy people, 
it is a biomarker.

## Metadata Column Exploration

### Column 1: Run
- Example: SRR1620013
- Purpose: Unique ID for each DNA sequencing file

### Column 2: BioSample
- Example: SAMN03025393
- Purpose: Links sample to patient clinical info

### Column 3: Organism
- Value: human skin metagenome
- Relevance: Studies ALL bacteria on skin together

### Column 4: isolation_source (Most Important)
- Example: Ac-L, Ph-R, N-R
- Ac = antecubital fossa = most common AD lesion site
- Ph = popliteal fossa = common AD lesion site
- Comparing these to dry sites identifies AD biomarkers

### Column 5: env_material
- Ss = Sebaceous, Sw = Sweaty, Cut = Cutaneous
- AD flares most common in sebaceous/moist areas

### Column 6: Collection_Date
- Range: June to October 2011
- Seasonal changes affect skin microbiome

### Column 7: host_sex
- Male/Female
- Hormones influence skin microbiome diversity

### Column 8: LibraryLayout
- Value: PAIRED
- Higher accuracy for bacterial classification

## Biological Conclusion
Body site (isolation_source) is the primary metadata 
variable linking this dataset to Atopic Dermatitis. 
Antecubital and popliteal sites show Staphylococcus 
aureus overgrowth and reduced microbial diversity — 
these are the key taxonomic biomarkers of AD.
